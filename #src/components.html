Some nanobots are designed to kill bacteria, but sometimes the bacteria themselves are the inspiration for nanobots’ development.

For example, many bacteria possess chemical rotors that enable them to power flagella or cilia, providing corkscrew or beading motions.

Such propulsion mechanism has inspired rotating synthetic microrobots, where synthetic helical microstructures, flexible filaments, or tumblers rotate in axis to that of a bacterial flagellum.

More than that, some magnetotactic bacteria with attached drug-containing nanoliposomes are used for drug delivery.

Graphical abstracts help grasp the idea of any paper through easy-to-digest visuals. In most cases, a graphical abstract is a single image representing a paper’s content concisely and vividly. It provides a visual equivalent of a textual abstract. Graphical abstracts are made to understand a text’s central message instantly. Their field of application covers scientific publications, journal articles, scholarly papers, research, reviews, product guides, and other documents and papers delivering knowledge.

Similarly to graphical abstracts, infographics utilize our ability to recognize trends and patterns in visual information. Infographics make sophisticated content easier to understand. Infographics improve our perception of data with visuals that come directly to our minds through our eyes. As a result, our cognition goes faster since information needs no translation into words. For instance, a graphical schematic of the
subway explains how to get to any station without any detailed textual description.

graphical abstracts?

Several types of graphical visuals are used for helpful infographics and graphical abstracts. They all enhance digesting knowledge-intensive information, whatever industry is concerned. // Trying out tools

Through research and sketching, an illustrator selects the most appropriate media tools and approaches to implement an illustration (traditional pen and paper or digital art, 3D tools or freehand sketching, etc.), which must be practiced beforehand to find a way to achieve the best results.

// Making up the composition

The general composition of the future illustration is drawn up based on the tools and media chosen, which may differ a lot. If approaches are primarily traditional, the illustrator transfers a pre-prepared element(s) or an entire composition onto the required final medium.

// Working up an illustration

For the most effort-intensive stage, an illustrator draws a complete medical illustration, which takes the most time and effort. This is because so many things are edited, reworked, and rendered a bunch of times in the process to chisel out the most satisfactory result.

// Finalizing the illustration

Finalizing a piece of art, be it medical or otherwise, takes a lot of nerve and skill. Good artists are rarely satisfied with their work, which is the main pitfall. The project may be scaled and improved for years to come, but with enough experience and a well-staged workflow, all the necessary edits are done in time, and the illustration is finished.

3D Animation

A vivid and conclusive story in 3D medical animation will find its way into the target audience's minds and stay there for a long time. 3D medical animations can be used to explain cellular and molecular processes, illustrate the function of medical devices, and highlight unique organ-level processes to demonstrate the action of a drug in the patient's body.

Which industries need 3D animation and why?

3D medical animation is actively used by our clients from the medical device, pharmaceutical, and biotechnology industries, universities and research centers, medical marketing companies, healthcare agencies, and investor relations companies.

The main areas in which our clients use 3D animation are Mechanism of Action (MoA) and Mechanism of Disease (MoD) videos, patient-centric communication, news studies, early stage concepts presentation, and medical training and learning.
